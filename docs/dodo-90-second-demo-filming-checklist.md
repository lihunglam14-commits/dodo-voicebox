# DoDo VoiceBox 90-Second Demo Filming Checklist

## Purpose

This checklist turns the approved 90-second storyboard into a practical filming
plan. It keeps DoDo positioned as a Cantonese smart companion and working
prototype exploring Hong Kong pilot collaboration.

Source storyboard:
`docs/dodo-90-second-product-demo-script-storyboard.md`

## Evidence and Disclosure Rules

Use one of these exact labels whenever the footage is not direct evidence of
confirmed prototype behaviour:

- `Prototype illustration` / `原型示意圖`
- `Illustrative scene` / `情境示意`
- `Staged demonstration` / `模擬示範`

Use `Controlled working-prototype demonstration` only when the recorded physical
prototype performs the behaviour shown in the same take or an honestly edited
sequence.

Apply these rules throughout:

- Do not present actors or staged scenes as user testing.
- Do not imply completed pilots, real pilot results, or confirmed NGO deployment.
- Do not present the public website or workflow display as the elderly-facing
  product interface.
- Do not imply emergency detection, guaranteed safety, clinical validation,
  medical monitoring, diagnosis, or automatic care decisions.
- Do not describe the hardware as production-ready or fully verified.
- Do not hide cuts that materially change what the prototype actually did.
- Keep `Working prototype` visible in the opening or closing product footage.

## Scene 1: Everyday Gap

**Time range:** 0-10 seconds

**Purpose:** Establish small everyday needs that may arise between calls and
service visits without suggesting an emergency.

**Narration summary:** An older adult may feel lonely, forget a small task, or
need help with an everyday concern.

**Required shots**

- Shot A: Wide shot of an older adult or actor in a warm, ordinary home setting.
- Shot B: Medium shot of a quiet daily routine, such as preparing tea or checking
  the time.
- Shot C: Close-up of a calendar, telephone, shopping note, or reminder card.
- Shot D: Optional brief shot of the person considering whom to contact.

**Props**

- Calendar or appointment card.
- Telephone.
- Shopping note and pen.
- Cup, kettle, or other neutral home objects.
- Medicine schedule card only; avoid identifiable medication or medical branding.

**Screen assets**

- Optional website text screenshot: `Everyday support between calls and visits`.
- No software interface is required.

**Footage classification**

- This scene can be staged.
- The actor must not be identified as a participant, pilot user, or service user.

**Disclosure label**

- Use `Illustrative scene` or `Staged demonstration`.

**Risk to avoid**

- Distress, falls, alarms, urgent symptoms, or imagery suggesting DoDo responds
  to emergencies.
- Claims that loneliness or care gaps have been measured or validated.

**Backup option**

- Use simple home-object close-ups with the `Illustrative scene` label.
- Use restrained animation based on the website colour palette.

## Scene 2: Meet DoDo

**Time range:** 10-25 seconds

**Purpose:** Introduce DoDo VoiceBox as a physical Cantonese smart companion and
friendly intelligent companion character.

**Narration summary:** Older adults can speak naturally to DoDo without apps or
touchscreens.

**Required shots**

- Shot A: Clean wide or medium shot of the real physical VoiceBox prototype.
- Shot B: Slow close-up showing its shape, logo, and confirmed physical details.
- Shot C: Older adult or actor sitting comfortably near the VoiceBox.
- Shot D: Brief website hero screen recording showing the DoDo brand and working
  prototype status.

**Props**

- Current physical VoiceBox prototype.
- Stable table or shelf at a comfortable height.
- Neutral home furniture and soft practical lighting.
- Cleaning cloth and safe power setup for the prototype.

**Screen assets**

- Homepage hero logo.
- `Working prototype` status.
- `A Cantonese smart companion` wording.
- `No apps or touchscreens` proof point.

**Footage classification**

- Real product footage is required to describe the object shown as the current
  physical prototype.
- A rendered or website visual may be used only with `Prototype illustration`.
- Website footage must not be described as the VoiceBox interface.

**Disclosure label**

- Real device: `Current working prototype` if factually accurate on filming day.
- Rendered visual: `Prototype illustration`.
- Staged home setup: `Staged demonstration`.

**Risk to avoid**

- Concealing unfinished hardware to imply a finished commercial product.
- Showing unconfirmed buttons, lights, microphones, or controls as functional.
- Calling the public landing page the product software.

**Backup option**

- Use the existing homepage hero visual with `Prototype illustration`.
- Pair it with the real logo and a text card stating `Physical prototype footage
  to be added after confirmation`.

## Scene 3: Speak, Respond, Remind

**Time range:** 25-45 seconds

**Purpose:** Demonstrate the intended Cantonese voice interaction and a simple
reminder.

**Narration summary:** An older adult speaks, DoDo replies in Cantonese, and a
simple reminder is provided.

**Required shots**

- Shot A: Older adult or actor says naturally: `DoDo，午飯後提我食藥。`
- Shot B: Close-up of the real VoiceBox while it receives the request.
- Shot C: Subtitle showing: `DoDo, remind me about my medicine after lunch.`
- Shot D: Confirmed Cantonese response from the prototype.
- Shot E: Confirmed reminder output or response at the appropriate demonstration
  point.
- Shot F: Cutaway showing only confirmed physical feedback, such as a light or
  sound that genuinely occurred.

**Props**

- Current VoiceBox prototype and confirmed power/network setup.
- Quiet recording space.
- External microphone for clean dialogue.
- Reminder cue sheet for continuity.
- Slate noting prototype version, date, and take number.

**Screen assets**

- Optional `Speak → Respond → Remind` title card.
- English and Traditional Chinese dialogue subtitles.
- Do not add a fabricated product dashboard.

**Footage classification**

- Real footage is required for `Controlled working-prototype demonstration`.
- Record the request and response with enough context to show what the prototype
  actually did.
- If the behaviour is simulated, reconstructed, or added in post-production,
  label the full sequence `Prototype illustration of intended interaction`.

**Disclosure label**

- Confirmed same-session behaviour: `Controlled working-prototype demonstration`.
- Actor performing the scenario: also add `Staged demonstration`.
- Animation or simulated response: `Prototype illustration`.

**Risk to avoid**

- Editing separate actions together in a way that overstates reliability.
- Suggesting always-on listening or verified hands-free wake behaviour.
- Presenting a medicine reminder as medical advice, adherence monitoring, or a
  safety guarantee.

**Backup option**

- Use a simple labelled voice-wave animation and subtitles.
- Show the physical prototype without claiming it generated the illustrated
  response.
- Label the complete sequence `Prototype illustration of intended interaction`.

## Scene 4: Simple Daily Needs

**Time range:** 45-60 seconds

**Purpose:** Show the kinds of ordinary reminders and needs the product direction
is designed to support.

**Narration summary:** DoDo may help an older adult express an appointment,
grocery, or medicine-collection need without diagnosing or making care decisions.

**Required shots**

- Shot A: Close-up of an appointment card.
- Shot B: Close-up of a short grocery list.
- Shot C: Actor says that collecting medicine is difficult this week.
- Shot D: Neutral text card: `Designed to support, not diagnose`.
- Shot E: Optional montage connecting each need to a simple spoken statement.

**Props**

- Generic appointment card with fictional details.
- Grocery list and reusable shopping bag.
- Generic medicine collection note with no personal or clinical data.
- Pen, calendar, and neutral household objects.

**Screen assets**

- Website illustrative journey wording.
- `Simple reminders and daily needs` title card.
- Privacy and limitations statement if room permits.

**Footage classification**

- Lifestyle shots can use `Illustrative scene`.
- Actor dialogue is a `Staged demonstration`.
- Any claim that DoDo captured or processed a need requires real confirmed
  prototype footage; otherwise use `Prototype illustration`.

**Disclosure label**

- `Illustrative scene` for daily-life inserts.
- `Staged demonstration` for actor dialogue.
- `Prototype illustration` for any animated processing or summary.

**Risk to avoid**

- Showing automatic detection of needs, risk, symptoms, or medication behaviour.
- Using real personal, health, or appointment information.
- Suggesting DoDo decides who should help.

**Backup option**

- Use animated appointment, grocery, and collection-note cards.
- Keep the wording at `may help express` and `designed to support`.

## Scene 5: Trusted Updates and Community Support

**Time range:** 60-75 seconds

**Purpose:** Explain consent-based, summary-level updates and human review.

**Narration summary:** With clear consent, trusted people may receive a simple
update and decide whether human or community support is appropriate.

**Required shots**

- Shot A: Screen recording or slow pan of the Phase 4A workflow display image.
- Shot B: Highlight the older adult-to-VoiceBox speech path.
- Shot C: Highlight the simple-update path to a trusted person or service team.
- Shot D: Show a human reviewing the information and making the decision.
- Shot E: End on community support as a possible connection, not an automatic
  outcome.

**Props**

- Laptop or tablet for displaying the partner-facing workflow image.
- Generic consent explanation sheet.
- Generic phone or printed summary for a trusted-person reaction shot.
- No real personal or care records.

**Screen assets**

- `public/assets/dodo-workflow-display-image-01.png`.
- Existing label: `Prototype workflow display for partners`.
- Existing caption explaining that older adults mainly use VoiceBox through
  speech and do not operate this interface.
- Optional website Privacy and Limitations screenshot.

**Footage classification**

- The workflow display is confirmed website material but remains a conceptual
  partner-facing display.
- Any trusted-person update screen that is not confirmed software must be a
  `Prototype illustration`.
- Human review can be a `Staged demonstration`.

**Disclosure label**

- Keep `Prototype workflow display for partners` visible with the image.
- Use `Prototype illustration` on unverified update screens.
- Use `Staged demonstration` for people reviewing a sample update.

**Risk to avoid**

- Presenting the workflow display as an elderly-facing interface.
- Showing unverified Care Console functionality as live software.
- Suggesting updates occur without consent or authorised access.
- Suggesting automatic escalation, guaranteed response, or emergency handling.

**Backup option**

- Use only the Phase 4A image with simple directional animation.
- Add: `Illustration of the intended consent and human-review flow`.

## Scene 6: Working Prototype and Pilot Collaboration

**Time range:** 75-90 seconds

**Purpose:** Close with honest product-stage positioning and a specific invitation
to explore a responsible pilot.

**Narration summary:** DoDo is not an emergency service or medical device. It is
a working prototype exploring Hong Kong pilot collaboration.

**Required shots**

- Shot A: Return to the real prototype or clearly labelled prototype illustration.
- Shot B: Website Evidence and Progress section showing `What exists now` and
  `What comes next`.
- Shot C: Website pilot section and `Explore a Pilot Collaboration` CTA.
- Shot D: Clean end card with logo, live website URL, and confirmed contact method.
- Shot E: Readable limitation line:
  `Not an emergency service or medical device`.

**Props**

- Physical VoiceBox prototype if available.
- Final approved contact details.
- Printed or digital QR code tested against the live website.

**Screen assets**

- Evidence and Progress section.
- `Working prototype` status.
- Pilot collaboration section and primary CTA.
- DoDo logo and live website URL.

**Footage classification**

- Website screen recordings are real public-site material.
- Physical prototype footage must represent the current device honestly.
- A rendered ending product shot requires `Prototype illustration`.

**Disclosure label**

- `Working prototype`.
- `Exploring Hong Kong pilot collaboration`.
- `Not an emergency service or medical device`.
- `Prototype illustration` if the final product visual is not real footage.

**Risk to avoid**

- Implying a pilot, partnership, deployment, funding relationship, or user result
  has already been confirmed.
- Describing the product as production-ready.
- Removing the limitation line to create a stronger sales claim.

**Backup option**

- Use the homepage hero and pilot sections followed by a branded text end card.
- Keep all working-prototype and limitation language visible.

## Website Screenshot and Screen-Recording Checklist

- [ ] Capture the live site at its current public URL.
- [ ] Hide browser bookmarks, account details, notifications, and unrelated tabs.
- [ ] Record at a resolution suitable for a 16:9 video master.
- [ ] Capture the hero with the full DoDo logo and working-prototype status.
- [ ] Capture the `Speak`, `Respond`, `Understand needs`, and `Connect` sequence.
- [ ] Capture the illustrative journey only if its qualification remains visible.
- [ ] Capture Evidence and Progress, including `What exists now` and
      `What comes next`.
- [ ] Capture the Phase 4A workflow image and its non-interface caption together.
- [ ] Capture Privacy and Limitations if used.
- [ ] Capture the pilot CTA and final contact area.
- [ ] Confirm the language shown matches the narration or subtitle version.
- [ ] Confirm no cursor, selection, loading state, missing asset, or console error
      appears in the final recording.
- [ ] Do not record internal dashboards, admin views, tokens, private data, or
      unverified software functionality.

## Voice-Over Recording Checklist

- [ ] Approve the final English and Traditional Chinese narration before recording.
- [ ] Record English and Cantonese/Traditional Chinese as separate clean tracks.
- [ ] Use a warm, calm, conversational delivery rather than an advertisement voice.
- [ ] Target approximately 90 seconds without rushing the limitation language.
- [ ] Pronounce `DoDo VoiceBox` consistently across both versions.
- [ ] Keep `may`, `designed to`, `with consent`, `working prototype`, and
      `exploring pilot collaboration` audible.
- [ ] Do not improvise claims about pilots, partners, safety, health, monitoring,
      reliability, or validation.
- [ ] Record at least two full takes and pickups for names, disclosures, and CTA.
- [ ] Capture 10 seconds of room tone.
- [ ] Export lossless master audio and a clearly named review copy.

## Subtitle Checklist

- [ ] Prepare separate English and Traditional Chinese subtitle files.
- [ ] Match subtitles to the approved narration, not an earlier draft.
- [ ] Subtitle all spoken prototype dialogue as well as the narrator.
- [ ] Keep disclosure labels on screen long enough to read.
- [ ] Avoid placing subtitles over the VoiceBox, faces, workflow labels, or CTA.
- [ ] Use high-contrast text and a size readable on mobile.
- [ ] Keep lines short and use natural phrase breaks.
- [ ] Verify Cantonese wording with a fluent reviewer.
- [ ] Check names, punctuation, arrows, and `DoDo VoiceBox` consistently.
- [ ] Export an accessible captioned version and retain subtitle source files.

## Consent and Release Checklist

- [ ] Obtain informed written consent before filming any identifiable person.
- [ ] State where and for how long the video may be published.
- [ ] Obtain permission for voice, likeness, location, and quoted dialogue.
- [ ] Confirm whether a participant may withdraw before publication.
- [ ] Clearly record whether each person is an actor, team member, or genuine
      participant.
- [ ] Do not identify an actor as a user, pilot participant, caregiver, or NGO worker.
- [ ] Do not film a genuine older adult participant without an appropriate,
      understandable consent process.
- [ ] Remove real names, health information, addresses, appointment details, and
      care records from all props and screens.
- [ ] Obtain releases for locations, music, fonts, photographs, and third-party media.
- [ ] Store signed releases separately from public production files.

## Safety and Claims Checklist

- [ ] Describe DoDo as a working prototype.
- [ ] Describe pilot work as being explored, not completed or confirmed.
- [ ] Keep the emergency-service and medical-device limitation visible.
- [ ] State that trusted updates require consent.
- [ ] Show human review before any support decision.
- [ ] Label staged and illustrative material at the point where it appears.
- [ ] Verify every product behaviour shown against the prototype version filmed.
- [ ] Do not imply emergency detection or guaranteed safety.
- [ ] Do not imply diagnosis, medical monitoring, clinical validation, or treatment.
- [ ] Do not imply automatic escalation or guaranteed service-team response.
- [ ] Do not imply completed user testing, pilot outcomes, deployment, or adoption.
- [ ] Do not imply confirmed NGO, university, advisor, funder, or partner support.
- [ ] Do not imply production-ready or fully verified hardware.
- [ ] Do not present the website, workflow display, or an illustration as functioning
      elderly-facing software.

## Final Pre-Filming Checklist

- [ ] Lock the script, storyboard, and this shot list to the same revision.
- [ ] Confirm which prototype behaviours are ready for real filming.
- [ ] Mark every planned shot as `real`, `staged`, or `illustrative`.
- [ ] Prepare the correct disclosure graphic for every staged or illustrative shot.
- [ ] Test the prototype under filming conditions without hiding failures.
- [ ] Record the prototype version, date, configuration, and known limitations.
- [ ] Test power, network, microphones, camera, storage, lighting, and backups.
- [ ] Prepare a shot slate and file-naming convention.
- [ ] Check that all props contain fictional information.
- [ ] Confirm all consent and release forms are signed.
- [ ] Test the final URL and QR code.
- [ ] Assign one person to monitor claims and continuity during filming.
- [ ] Prepare the illustration-only backup for any unready prototype behaviour.

## Final Post-Filming Review Checklist

- [ ] Compare every scene against the approved storyboard and narration.
- [ ] Confirm the final duration is close to 90 seconds without rushed disclosures.
- [ ] Confirm real prototype footage shows only behaviour that actually occurred.
- [ ] Confirm editing does not combine takes in a misleading way.
- [ ] Confirm all staged and illustrative sequences have the correct visible label.
- [ ] Confirm actors are not presented as tested users or pilot participants.
- [ ] Confirm the workflow display remains labelled as partner-facing.
- [ ] Confirm consent and human review are explicit in Scene 5.
- [ ] Confirm the closing limitation and working-prototype status are readable.
- [ ] Run a claim review for emergency, medical, clinical, monitoring, deployment,
      partnership, validation, and hardware-readiness implications.
- [ ] Review English and Traditional Chinese narration and subtitles independently.
- [ ] Check the video on desktop and mobile for subtitle and disclosure readability.
- [ ] Confirm the website URL, QR code, logo, and contact method are correct.
- [ ] Confirm no private data, credentials, browser chrome, or internal software
      screens are visible.
- [ ] Obtain final approval from the product owner before publishing or changing
      the website demo placeholder.
