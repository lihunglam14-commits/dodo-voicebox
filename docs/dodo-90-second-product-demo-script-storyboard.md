# DoDo VoiceBox 90-Second Product Demo Script and Storyboard

## Purpose and Positioning

This video introduces DoDo VoiceBox to professors, NGOs, funders, elderly centres,
and community-care teams. It presents DoDo as:

- a Cantonese smart companion and intelligent companion character;
- a friendly voice companion for older adults;
- a working prototype exploring Hong Kong pilot collaboration.

The video must not imply that DoDo is a medical device, emergency service,
clinical monitoring system, deployed service, finished commercial product, or
replacement for professional or human care.

## Timed Storyboard

### Scene 1: The Everyday Gap (0-10 seconds)

**English narration**

Between family calls and service visits, an older adult may still feel lonely,
forget a small task, or need help with an everyday concern.

**Traditional Chinese narration**

在家人來電和服務探訪之間，長者仍可能感到孤單、忘記一件小事，或遇到日常需要而不知道向誰開口。

**Suggested on-screen text**

- EN: `Everyday support between calls and visits`
- TC: `在來電與探訪之間提供日常支援`

**Suggested visuals**

- A warm, quiet home environment with an older adult carrying out a normal
  morning routine.
- Brief details such as a calendar, telephone, shopping note, or medicine
  schedule.
- Avoid distress imagery or anything suggesting an emergency.

**Existing website material**

- The unmet-need statement about small daily needs between calls and visits.
- The warm cream-and-green visual palette.

## Missing Assets Before Filming

### Required for an evidence-based demo

- Real product photography of the current physical VoiceBox prototype.
- Confirmed footage of the prototype completing a controlled Cantonese voice
  exchange and simple reminder.
- A clean, approved Cantonese DoDo voice sample.
- Confirmation of which physical lights, sounds, or controls can be shown.
- A verified representation of any simple update received by a trusted person;
  otherwise use a clearly labelled prototype illustration.
- Final pilot contact details and the URL or QR code for the end card.

### Production requirements

- Older adult performer or participant, with informed filming and usage consent.
  Staged footage must not be presented as user testing.
- A suitable home location and simple props for reminders and daily needs.
- English and Cantonese voice-over recordings.
- English and Traditional Chinese subtitle files.
- Licensed music, if music is used.
- Release forms for people, locations, voices, and any third-party footage.

### Evidence not to imply until verified

- Completed user testing or pilot outcomes.
- Confirmed NGO, university, advisor, or funding relationships.
- Reliable hands-free wake behavior.
- Fully verified commercial hardware.
- Emergency detection, clinical validation, or medical monitoring.

## Website Wording After the Demo Is Produced

Only after the final demo has been reviewed against the real prototype:

- Replace `90-second product demo coming next` with
  `Watch the 90-second product demo`.
- Label the video `Recorded working-prototype demonstration` only if the footage
  shows confirmed product behavior.
- If live action, animation, and prototype footage are mixed, add:
  `Some scenes are illustrative. Product functions shown are identified in the video.`
- Add a transcript and captions in English and Traditional Chinese.
- Add the recording month and prototype version so viewers understand the
  product stage.
- Keep `Not an emergency service or medical device` near the video or in its
  accessible description.
- Do not add partner, pilot, user-feedback, or validation claims until those
  materials have been confirmed.
